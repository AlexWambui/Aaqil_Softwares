General Guidelines

What is Trax?

TraX is Verbit’s next-generation platform, utilizing the power of teamwork to provide a near real-time transcript. Multiple transcribers work on the same job together, each assigned short tasks, called segments.

Overall responsibility

  • Confirm your attendance
  • Access the job via a link that will be sent to you on Slack
  • Get a short audio segment to transcribe
  • Listen to the audio and quickly correct errors with no research and separate speakers
  • Verify names, locations, and professional terms with the glossary (and add terms, if needed)
  • Mark inaudibles
  • Go over a second time, if possible
  • Publish before time is up

General Guidelines

Before the session

  1. If you can't attend one of the sessions, inform us on the #trax_group
  2. You'll be asked if you're joining the session 1 hour to 30 mins before the session in the Slack Channel #trax_group

Accessing the job

  • login to your regular platform
  • Sessions start at the scheduled time. Sometimes there's delays.
  • Once the session is ready, you'll be asked to join by accessing the job via link provided in the slack channel.

Training Session

  • must attend first 10 sessions after the webinar (scheduled: mon, Wed, Fri (1:00PM UTC))
  • First session is for you to "play around" and get to know the platform. User all shortcuts, add and edit glossary terms, play around with all context menu options, etc.
  • Next 9 sessions are reviewed and feedback is sent. Implement feedback and improve accordingly.
  • After 10 sessions, it's decided if you're qualified or not and you're updated on slack.

Practice Sessions

  1. Once qualified, you're expected to attend at least three practice sessions per week.
  2. Additional demo and live sessions available as well and invitations are sent once in a while.

Shortcuts:

  • Ctrl + SPACE - play or pause
  • Ctrl + <- / -> - rewind / forward audio by 2 seconds
  • Ctrl + Shift + <- / Ctrl + Shift + -> - highlight word(s)
  • Ctrl + z / Ctrl + Y - undo / redo
  • Ctrl + c / Ctrl + v - copy / paste
  • Ctrl + G - open the glossary
  • Ctrl + D - context menu(tags)
  • Ctrl + R - open verbit's search tool to learn about a word
  • Alt + S - go to beginning of the segment
  • Alt + > - increase speed
  • Alt + < - decrease speed
  • Alt + ? - mark as unclear or inaudible
  • Alt + p - publish
  • Alt + S - replay task

Special Situation: no text in segment?

means the AI did not pick up any words. It could be that there's music, no sound, or an error. Listen to the audio and add text if the AI missed it. If there is no speech, publish the segment empty.

Special Situation: witness does not speak English?

When a witness doesn’t speak English they will have an interpreter. In this case, please delete all the text from the foreign language paragraph and add an unclear tag. Please note that the interpreter's paragraphs should be transcribed (as a separate speaker).

Glossary

Gets rid of need for researching outside of the Trax platform.

Principal names, addresses and professional terms will be provided in the Glossary.

use and updates the Glossary terms to provide consistency throughout the transcript, assist other transcribers to resolve unclears, and provide a higher quality transcript. Update all names, locations, and terms according to the glossary.

Unclears / Inaudibles

there's two cases:

  • If a word is unclear (you're not sure but you can guess) - Highlight the work then click Alt + ?
  • If inaudible (you don't understand or can't guess) - add space between inaudible text and click Alt + ? (without highlighting any words)

Transcription Guidelines

General Guidelines

These are guidelines applied to all transcriptions.

Two types of guidelines: Clean Read and Verbatim.

Job-specific guidelines take precedence.

Clean Read Overview

ensures transcript is concise and clear, without interruptions.

omits any stutters, filler words, interruptions, partial words and false starts.

transcriptions should be free of slang, irrelevant interjections and non-verbal communications

And should not begin a sentence. Either omit it or connect 2 sentences using and in between.

maintain meaning of the sentence.

words like so at the start of a sentence are not always filler words. if a speaker begins many thoughts with so, you can likely omit it. However, if the word so could be replaced with therefore, and maintain the meaning, leave it in.

no need to expand words like it's, that's, etc, you've, you'll and more of this sort.

when describing interface buttons and menu buttons, ensure you capitalize them and use double quotes. Tabs and page names should be capitalized. Double quotes should only be used when describing an action of clicking a button.

Example: Now that we're inside the extension, I'm going to press "Upload" and choose the file that I want to upload and click "Okay". Then come back to the Install page.

Verbatim Overview

includes all dialogue spoken, word by word, including filler, partial words, false starts, incorrect grammar, slang, stutters and repetitions.

ensure everything the speaker utters is written.

For stutters, partial words and false starts, use a dash.

  • Stutter: I- I- I'm going shopping
  • Partial word: close the window, I', fr- freezing.
  • False start: are you hungry for- there's a great new place on main street.
