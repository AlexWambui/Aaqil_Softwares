Computing Units of measure

The measures for comparing various aspects of the computer involve the size or capacity of some aspect of the machine or the speed of the machine.

Speed shows how long a computer takes to do some operations.

Current computers do most arithmetic operations in nanoseconds but are expected to do operations in picoseconds in the future.

Fractions of seconds:

  • 1/1000 - millisecond (ms)
  • 1/1 000 000 - microsecond (US)
  • 1/1 000 000 000 - nanosecond (ns)
  • 1/1 000 000 000 000 - picosecond (ps)

Apart the measure of speed, the clock rate is also used.

Clock rate is how many operations the computer can perform in a second and is expressed as cycles per second or hertz.

The higher the clock rate, the faster the computer.

eg. A computer with a clock rate of 50, 000, 000 hertz does something every 20 ns. (ie. 1 / 50, 000, 000).

Clock rate values in hertz:

  • 1000 - Kilo (K)
  • 1 000 000 - Mega (M)
  • 1 000 000 000 - Giga (G)
  • 1 000 000 000 000 - Tetra (T)

In terms of measure of capacity or size, computer scientists prefer to count things using powers of two.

This system is convenient because digital computers use the binary number system.

Abbreviation for 210 is K, from the stem "Kilo".

Prefix "Kilo" is used because 1024 is closest to 1000.

A machine with 8 mb of memory has 8 x 2020 bytes or 8 388 608 bytes of memory.

Memory sizes in bytes represented as powers of 2.

  • 210 - 1 024 (Kilo - K)
  • 220 - 1 048 576 (Mega - M)
  • 230 - 1 073 741 824 (Giga - G)
  • 240 - 1 099 511 627 776 (Tera - T)
