Introduction

Slack is a messaging app for business that connects people with the information they need.

It brings people together to work as one unified team and transforms the way organisations comunicate.

Connected - People can work in dedicated spaces called channels that bring together the right people and information.

Flexible - when work is organized into channels, no matter your location, time zone or function, you can access the information you need on your own time.

Channels

Slack organizes conversations into dedicated spaces called channels.

Channels bring order and clarity to work - you can create then for any project, topic or team.

With the right people and information in one place, teams can share ideas, make decisions and move work forward.

The #general channel

Every Slack workspace has a #general channel, which is the only channel that members (but not guests) are automatically added to and unable to leave.

This channel works best when it's used for sharing workspace-wide announcements.

The channel can be archived, deleted or converted to a private channel.

Threads

Threads help create an organized discussion around a specific message.

They let you discuss a topic in more detail without adding clutter to a channel or direct message (DM) conversation.

Huddle

Is a lightweight audio call that lets you and members of your team talk to each other in real-time.

A way for peers to have informal audio conversations just like they would in a physical office setting.

A good reason to start a huddle would be:

  • To help out a peer with a task or a project.
  • To do a manual review for a project which requires asking questions.
  • Pair programming - peer learning.

A huddle can hold around 50 people.
