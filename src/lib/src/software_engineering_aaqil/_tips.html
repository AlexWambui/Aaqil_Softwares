Learning a new language

THE MORE SKILLS YOU ACQUIRE THE MORE CONFIDENT YOU BECOME.

  1. Don't try to learn too fast - Real accomplishment is when you learn things with precisions. So it's more important to learn with perfection. The early stage is for learning the basics of the language as it sets the foundation (Variables, functions, conditions, loops, classes, objects).
  2. Don't learn things randomly - Focus on one thing at a time. Try to learn one thing at a time to ensure it sticks. Figure out a roadmap to follow. Go through a bootcamp. This helps to know what to learn and when to learn it.
  3. Don't just memorize - Coding is an applicable skill, not just a collection of abstract facts.
  4. Check up example codes - Best way of learning is self learning. Here you can also consult various text books where examples are provided.
  5. More and more practice - Practice makes perfect. The old school thought matches the conditions of programming.
  6. Learn using a debugger - This is more effective as it helps find possible chances or errors more easily.
  7. Be an active learner - Change your approach of learning. Online tutorials can help you follow methodical process. Don't just consume video tutorials. Try out things you learn on yourself without help.
  8. Innovate and build stuff - Learning is just the beginning. Jump in and do your best. Have tangible goals. Build your own projects as it helps you learn more. Build a portfolio to demonstrate your ability to code.
  9. Market - Put yourself out there. Build a Linkedin, message recruiters, apply for jobs, work with people, go for interviews.

Sabotages to coding career

  1. Trying to hide your mistakes - Tell your project manager or boss right away so they can inform clients or come up with game plans to fix it. Transparency is usually respected so speak out when you make mistakes so they can be corrected.
  2. Not asking for help - Ask for help even though it might be hard. Ask why solutions work when you get help so you know how to deal with the issue later.
  3. Being defensive and close-minded - Don't complain when you meet with challenges. Have an open and growth mindset. Deal with projects that are out of your comfort zone. Be a good team player and always to research.

Becoming a self taught developer

  1. Figure out your end goal
    • Work for a company, start up or freelance?
    • Game development, Data science, mobile apps, Web development: Frontend or backend?
    • What are you good at? Maths (data science), Design (Web and app dev)
    • Check (payscales, job security) for different types of jobs.
  2. Choose a programming language
    • which language to learn for what you want to do.
    • work on fundamentals first (frameworks later)
    • choose a text editor / IDE
    • switch to another language if need be.
  3. Find resources and build a curriculum
    • books are best for fundamentals
    • documentaries are good for reference
    • websites include: w3schools and MDB web docs
    • Video courses: Youtube, Udemy, Pluralsight, Lynda, Coursera
    • Online bootcamps - Freecodecamp, codeacademy, team treehouse (paid)
    • challenge websites - codewars
  4. Find a community (Don't be anti-social)
    • having people to talk to and learn from is very beneficial
    • Find a mentor if possible
    • Join an online community
    • Go to meetups
  5. Create your own projects
    • Following along with courses is only half the battle.
    • It's where most people get stuck and many quit.
    • Projects don't have be from scratch.
    • Add new features, use the docs as a supplement to figure out new stuff.
    • Look at other people's code that's similar to what you want to achieve.
    • Start creating your projects from scratch.
  6. Create your portfolio
    • create an attractive but simple portfolio
    • Showcase a live version (if possible) and your source code
    • self taught devs need a great portfolio with work samples
  7. Contribute to open source
    • Gives you experience with real life projects
    • Collaborate with other devs
    • Have something for your own resume
    • Learn how to use github
  8. Create an online presence
    • important for self-taught devs with no degree
    • Have an up to date linkedin and professional twitter
    • Upload all your work to Github and keep it well documented
    • write articles, tutorials, videos
    • the more you are visible on the web, the better
  9. Prepare and apply for jobs
    • prepare for interviews, practice algorithms, create a resume, cover letter (etc)
    • search for jobs in your area that are looking for things you know
    • apply even if it says degree required
    • remember its you first job, not your last
